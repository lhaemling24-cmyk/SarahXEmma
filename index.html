Introduction

Can students today still afford the same leisure opportunities?

Emma and Sarah are two sisters from Hamburg. While Sarah started her studies in 2016, Emma is currently pursuing her bachelor's degree in "Media and Communication " since 2021. Both have a great passion for sports, coffee drinking, and spending time with their friends. A nearly identical beginning. However, over the years, the financial situation for students has changed significantly.

Can students like Emma still afford the same leisure opportunities today as Sarah did five years ago?

What role does child benefit play?

Besides income from student jobs and parental support, child benefit represents an important part of many students' monthly budgets.

Finding a place to live in Hamburg has become much harder for students. Between 2016 and 2024, rents for all types of student housing increased.

This means that during the time between Sarah starting university and Emma looking for a place to live, housing became much more expensive. The biggest increase was for rooms in shared apartments.

The chart compares three types of housing using an index (2016 = 100): student dormitories run by the German Student Services, 35-square-metre apartments on the housing market, and rooms in shared apartments.

Student dormitories are still the most affordable option, but their rents have also gone up. Shared apartments have become much more expensive and rents on the general housing market have also continued to rise.

For Emma, this means entering a housing market that is much more expensive than the one Sarah faced when she first moved to Hamburg. Overall, the data shows that finding affordable student housing has become increasingly difficult over the years.

It isn’t just rent that got more expensive…

many leisure activities nowadays cost a larger share of a student’s budget

When people talk about affordability, rent usually gets the most attention. But after paying for housing, students still want to go to the cinema, meet friends for a drink or keep their gym membership.

These activities have also become considerably more expensive over the past years. To understand what changed, we compare the development of leisure prices with Hamburg’s overall consumer price index.

What changed?

Most leisure activities became more expensive over the past years, but not to the same extent.

While Hamburg's overall Consumer Price Index increased steadily, some leisure activities rose much more sharply.

Everything became more expensive…

The most striking example is the döner, whose price increased by around 75% compared with 2016. Cinema tickets and alcoholic drinks also became noticeably more expensive, whereas gym memberships experienced a much smaller increase.

Key takeaway

The important takeaway isn't that every price increased equally.
It's that students today like Emma need to spend a larger share of their budget on the same everyday leisure activities than students like Sarah did in 2016.

However, looking at prices alone doesn't tell the whole story.

The important question isn't how much activities cost

The Good News

Having less money available doesn't mean giving up student life.

Throughout this comparison we have seen that Emma has less money available for leisure activities than Sarah did in 2016.

But affordability isn't only determined by prices and budgets. Hamburg offers numerous free and low-cost opportunities that allow students to stay active, social and engaged without putting additional pressure on their budget.
